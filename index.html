Seeing Through Masters’ Eyes

- Experiencing the Creation of Masterpiece in Virtual Reality

Museums are challenged for a long time to engage their visitors. Many forms of technologies have been adapted for propose of engaging people by providing more information. Virtual reality has been studied for in museums for cultural propose for several years. The technology promises to offer a vivid environment for users to interact within in a first-person perspective. This project is proposing a virtual reality experience based on the statues of St. Longinus in St. Peter’s Basilica by Bernini. Extending from previous projects, this project will let users to experience how the piece was made from the artist’s perspective. The experience would also incorporate physical replica of the tools that users could interact with and to increase immersion and more deeply connect users to the artist’s process using haptic feedback. For user input, several gesture capture devices will be studied to bring an intuitive way of interaction to users.

Jump In!

Jump In is an interactive physical exercising game for children 5 – 10 years old. The game is a procedural game that can adjust the pace of the game according to the user’s heart rate. Users will use a Kinect camera to interact with the game and a wearable device will be wore by the user to capture real time pulse data.

This is a group project. And I was in charge of 3D modeling, communication between Arduino and unity3D (using C# and arduino), and building landing page. I also co-worked on project managing and user interaction design.

Interactive Performances

Legend of the Sun

Alice in Wonderland



Alice in Wonderland is an interactive dance performance piece as well. This project includes interactive lights, and background animation. The background animation was splinted into different phrases according to the story and generated by processing. The images used in all the animation was made/edited in Photoshop. We had a spotlight that followed the dancer and LED lights that change colours according to scenes. I was responsible for the interactive light and making the animation assets.

Legend of the Sun is an interactive dance project combines dance with animation. This is a group project made by Will Shengyi Li and myself. We used Kinect camera to capture the dancer’s movement and mapped our elements in the animation to follow her around. I was in charge of the interaction design, animation, and sound design. The background was drawn in Photoshop then animated in After Effects and Final Cut. I also worked as a technical support for the MaxMSP patch.

Physical Computing Projects

Robotic Hand

CityBeat

Robotic hand project is based on a project from Instructables. It has two parts – a glove with haptic feedback and control sensors, and a robotic hand that imitates the gesture from the glove and sends out signal once someone touches it. It is an Arduino based project that used 2 types of sensors (flex and pressure) and 2 types of motors (servo and vibration).

CityBeat is a collaborative project made in Hack’n’Talk 2015. It is an interactive dress that can change colour according to the data we got from Toronto Open Data. We used an Arduino as our controller and several RGB LEDs connecting to fiber-optic threads to make the effect. I worked as the coder in the team and was responsible for writing the processing code that read and parse the XML file from Toronto Open Data then send the data to Arduino.
